[Weekly Issue] Daishin's View

Key Factor Outlook

Interest Rate

The bond market is currently dominated by fear, expecting excessive interest rate hikes. Following the conflict with Iran, concerns over high oil prices and inflation have dampened expectations for interest rate cuts, and even fueled anxieties about potential rate increases. This fear is currently driving the market into oversold territory, despite assessments that interest rates may already be excessively high.

In Korea, concerns about interest rate hikes have rapidly spread following the nomination of a new central bank governor. However, interest rates have already risen to a level that reflects these potential increases, leading to a consensus that the market is in oversold territory.

Target Range for 3-Year Korean Treasury Bond: 3.20% ~ 3.70%

Exchange Rate

The current market is driven by geopolitical risks, with heightened volatility in the foreign exchange market due to the US-Iran conflict. The prolonged conflict and soaring international oil prices have exposed the dollar-won exchange rate to upward pressure, settling around 1,500 won.

While a rollback in the exchange rate is likely if the situation de-escalates, there is a possibility of overshooting repeatedly depending on the war's developments in the coming 2-3 weeks, as indicated by a recent statement from Trump.

Dollar-Won Exchange Rate Outlook: 1,400 ~ 1,530 KRW/USD

Domestic Stock Market

Geopolitical risks, coupled with concerns about a slowdown in semiconductor demand, have pushed the KOSPI's forward PER to below 8x. This presents an opportunity for a proactive increase in investment.

We recommend strengthening an overweight strategy at KOSPI levels around 5,000 points, with its forward PER below 8x. We anticipate the easing of geopolitical risks in the Middle East during April. Concerns about semiconductor demand slowdown due to turbo quant are deemed excessive.

Furthermore, a reinforced policy drive, improved earnings outlook during the 1Q earnings season, and a clear upward trend in forward EPS are expected. Once geopolitical risks subside, a resumption of the upward trend is anticipated, driven by fundamental momentum and attractive valuations.

KOSPI Outlook: 5,000 ~ 7,500 points

Global Stock Market

A relief rally is expected once geopolitical tensions in the Middle East ease. Excluding the war variable, the investment environment is supportive of a risk-on sentiment.

Expectations for de-escalation of tensions in the Middle East are growing, with the US setting an end-of-war deadline of April 9th and engaging in negotiations with Iran. Despite inflation concerns stemming from the war, expected inflation remains stable, and consistent corporate earnings growth, particularly among Big Tech, is noteworthy.

Even if the war ends, the impact of disrupted energy supply is expected to persist for at least another 1-2 months. This will lead to a decrease in trade balance and increased costs, impacting corporate earnings. Countries that are net energy exporters are favored in this environment.

S&P 500 Outlook: 6,400 ~ 7,700 points

Commodities

Trump has warned of an attack on Iran's Kark Island within the next 2-3 weeks. However, considering the War Powers Act, a conclusion to the conflict this month is possible.

President Trump has warned of potential attacks on Kark Island, which accounts for 90% of Iran's oil exports, within the next 2-3 weeks if Iran does not comply with US demands, reiterating the ongoing conflict and increasing uncertainty. However, considering the War Powers Act, the conflict may conclude this month. Obama continued the Libya war in 2011 citing the absence of ground troops, but this action was taken after the mid-term elections. For Trump, facing mid-term elections, this presents a significant burden.

WTI Outlook: 95 ~ 115 $/bbl

Real Estate

The adjustment in the Gangnam area driven by multi-homeowners selling properties appears to be concluding. Further adjustments may occur depending on future policy directions.

Residential: The adjustment related to capital gains tax for multi-homeowners is nearing its end. However, depending on the tax changes for single-homeowners, an increase in supply may occur after May. Demand-driven price increases are expected to continue in mid-to-low price areas with high Jeonse rates. Commercial: Investment attractiveness is decreasing due to rising market interest rates and a slowdown in the office rental market. However, with ample liquidity, demand is expected to concentrate on large-scale, new, and prime-location properties.

Residential Outlook: (Conditional) Stable Commercial Outlook: Rising

Domestic Stock Market Outlook and Top 10 Korean Stocks

The KOSPI's 12-month forward PER has reached the Covid-19 low point at 7.8x. Could Samsung Electronics' earnings guidance be a turning point?

Despite the ongoing geopolitical risks in the Middle East, policy and earnings momentum are strengthening. Notably, with the commencement of the 2026 1Q pre-earnings season, earnings forecasts and forward EPS are showing a clear upward trend. Forward EPS has leveled up to 684p in April (611.6p at the end of February, 666.6p at the end of March).

As a result, the KOSPI forward PER has decreased to 7.86x, reaching levels comparable to the Covid-19 lows, indicating a "Deep Value" phase. Even if uncertainties in the economy and earnings increase with a time lag, a normalization of valuation is expected at minimum.

This week, Samsung Electronics will release its earnings guidance on the 7th. The consensus over the past month has exceeded 40 trillion won, indicating strong earnings expectations. The normalization of KOSPI valuations is expected to accelerate depending on the earnings results of semiconductors, a notably undervalued sector.

Global Stock Market Outlook and Top 10 Overseas Stocks

Focus on the existing investment environment once the Iran conflict subsides. The currency and fiscal policies of various countries in response to Trump's tariffs are factors increasing liquidity.

The Iran conflict has lasted over four weeks, with the petrochemical value chain showing visible damage due to rising commodity prices. Even after the conflict ends, the disruption to energy supply is expected to persist for at least another 1-2 months, inevitably impacting the real economy negatively.

Stock markets in countries highly dependent on Middle Eastern energy (Europe, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, India, etc.) saw larger declines following the Iran conflict. In contrast, stock markets in net energy exporting countries (US, Canada, Brazil, etc.) experienced limited declines.

In the coming period, countries with high energy dependence will continue to face concerns about fundamental deterioration, including declining trade balances, currency instability, and reduced corporate earnings due to rising costs. Therefore, stock markets in net energy exporting countries are expected to shine relatively brighter during the upcoming relief rally in April.

The impact of energy supply disruptions will persist even after the Iran conflict ends. When a relief rally occurs, net energy exporting countries will be highlighted.

Interest Rate Outlook and Major Bond-Related Products

Concerns about interest rate hikes due to high oil prices are gradually subsiding. Inflationary pressures stemming from supply-side factors like oil prices are difficult to counter with interest rate hikes. While there was a somewhat one-sided expectation for policy responses, the realization that inflationary pressures are also detrimental to economic conditions has led to a normalization of monetary policy expectations in the bond market.

In Korea, concerns about interest rate hikes following the nomination of the new governor are also showing signs of calming. Furthermore, the Korean government bond market is seeing foreign buying driven by its inclusion in the WGBI index, suggesting a move towards stabilizing market rates. After entering oversold territory, a rebound in market interest rates is expected to continue for some time.

Foreign Exchange and Commodity Outlook

The exchange rate has settled around the 1,500 won level due to persistent geopolitical and energy market instability. At the beginning of the week, the dollar-won rate briefly overshot to the mid-1,530 won level as concerns about the prolonged war intensified and regulatory vigilance weakened.

The US exit strategy is becoming increasingly visible. However, Trump has indicated the possibility of inflicting "severe damage" on Iran within the next 2-3 weeks, suggesting that currency overshooting may occur in the coming period depending on the war's developments until mid-April. After the energy supply issue is resolved, a return to the 1,400 won range is expected.

President Trump has warned of attacking Iran's Kark Island within 2-3 weeks if Iran does not comply with US demands. However, considering the War Powers Act, a cessation of hostilities within April is possible. Obama continued the Libya war in 2011, citing the absence of ground troops, but this action was taken after the mid-term elections. For Trump, this presents a significant burden before the mid-term elections.

Even if the Strait of Hormuz reopens, the problem lies in seasonality. Middle Eastern oil-producing countries, where 40% of their electricity is generated by oil, face the cooling season from April to September. Even if exports resume, normalization is expected to take 2-3 months.
